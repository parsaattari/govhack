United States Refugee Admissions Program:

Who does America define as a refugee:

a person of a certain nationality that is unable to return to that person's country in which they habitually resided due to a lack of protection or persection/actual fear of persection based on race, religion, nationality, membership in a particular social group, or political opinion or political opinion, or any person the President specifies with consultations of circumstances. Anyone who has ordered, incited, assisted, or otherwise participated in the persecution of any person on account of race, religion, nationality, membership in a particular social group, or political opinion will no longer be considered a refugee.

The application process:
  • First, you must recieve a referral to the U.S. Refugee Admissions Program.

  • Then you must meet the following criteria as to be determined from an interview with a UCIS officer that will come to your location:
    • qualified under a designated processing priority.
    • meets the definition of a refugee.
    • is not firmly resettled in a third country.
    • is otherwise admissible under U.S. law.

    You may include spouse and in some circumstances other family members in your application.

    ** There is no fee to apply for refugee status
